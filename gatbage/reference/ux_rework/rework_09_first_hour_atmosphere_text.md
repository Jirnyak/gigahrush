# rework_09_first_hour_atmosphere_text

Target model: GPT-5.5 worker.

Mode: implementation/text worker. Russian player-facing text is canonical. Do not translate existing Russian by accident.

## Goal

Improve first-hour atmosphere by making text concrete, local and useful, not by adding more random chatter.

The world already has strong material. The weak point is which lines reach the player and whether those lines contain action, price, risk, place or consequence.

## Feedback This Addresses

- Atmosphere does not feel hopeless enough.
- NPC lines can feel generic or noisy.
- Chat/log moves too fast.
- Players want more character, but less interruption.

## Tone Contract

Follow `scenarist.md`.

Good ГИГАХРУЩ text usually contains at least one:

- person
- place
- object
- action
- price
- risk
- reward
- consequence
- joke
- procedure

Bad text is abstract horror wallpaper.

## Atmosphere Direction

"Безнадёга" should come from consequences:

- not enough shelter places
- medpost has one dose and a queue
- someone did not return after samosbor
- a door only fits some people
- debt survives longer than people
- fake medicine still sells
- children count who came back
- repair worker knows one gasket will not save the whole block

Do not make every line grim. Normal life makes horror sharper.

## First Pass Domains

Prioritize:

1. Olga/Barni/Yakov starter clarity.
2. Citizens/domkom/local queue speech.
3. Medics and quarantine.
4. Liquidators and repair workers.
5. Traders/debt/black market.
6. Children/old people/shelter witnesses.
7. Ambient bark pools only after channels are triaged.

## Text Routing

Coordinate with `rework_04`:

- HUD: short survival/consequence lines only.
- Log: local heard speech and event history.
- Talk: longer character and rumor lines.
- Rumors: delayed social consequences.

Do not add more HUD text to solve atmosphere.

- More barks without signal classification.

## Acceptance Criteria

- First NPC text tells the player what to do and why.
- First-hour text has more concrete survival/social consequences.
- Ambient barks are not louder than quest/survival facts.
- No broad localization regression.
- Tone follows `scenarist.md`: concrete, local, adult, not trailer-poetic.
