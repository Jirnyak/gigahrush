## Title

GIGAH|RUSH - free browser survival horror in an endless concrete apartment block

## Body HTML

Use this version when editing the itch.io forum post through the rich editor. The old post failed because itch displayed Markdown literally.

```html
<p><img src="https://img.itch.zone/aW1hZ2UvNDU4NzE2MC8yNzQwNDQ4OS5naWY=/original/LTioNh.gif" alt="GIGAH|RUSH gameplay"></p>

<p>Hi. I just launched <strong>GIGAH|RUSH</strong>, a free browser survival horror about expeditions inside an endless Soviet-style concrete apartment block.</p>

<p>You prepare food, water, ammo, bandages, documents and weapons, then leave the safer living area for hostile floors with factions, traders, monsters, rumors, quests and shifting routes.</p>

<p>The world keeps moving while you play. NPCs trade, sleep, fight, hide from Samosbor events and can die permanently. Factions react to what you do, zones change control, market prices shift, and consequences remain in the world.</p>

<p><strong>Samosbor</strong> is the main threat: doors can seal, fog pushes through cracks, monsters become active, and the floor can change after the alarm ends.</p>

<p>The current build already includes:</p>

<ul>
  <li>preparation and expeditions;</li>
  <li>combat, trading, inventory and quests;</li>
  <li>factions, reputation and market pressure;</li>
  <li>procedural and hand-made floors;</li>
  <li>browser saves;</li>
  <li>A-Life NPCs and persistent consequences.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Play in browser on itch.io:</strong><br>
<a href="https://tenevik.itch.io/gigahrush">https://tenevik.itch.io/gigahrush</a></p>

<p><strong>Direct online build:</strong><br>
<a href="https://gigahrush.bileter.workers.dev">https://gigahrush.bileter.workers.dev</a></p>

<p>I would especially appreciate feedback on expedition pacing, UI readability, survival pressure and whether Samosbor feels dangerous enough.</p>
```
